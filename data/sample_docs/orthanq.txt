Orthanq: Uncertainty-Aware Haplotype Quantification

Orthanq is a Bayesian inference framework, implemented in Rust, designed for haplotype
quantification from high-throughput sequencing data. Unlike point-estimate approaches,
Orthanq explicitly models uncertainty arising from noisy, incomplete, or ambiguous
measurements, producing calibrated probability distributions over candidate haplotypes
rather than single best guesses.

The core inference engine combines probabilistic modeling with Linear Programming (LP)
and Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) formulations to search the space of feasible
haplotype combinations efficiently. Several algorithmic strategies were explored to keep
runtime tractable on large, high-dimensional datasets, including recursive enumeration
algorithms and graph-based data structures that prune infeasible branches early.

A key application area is HLA typing, where accurate haplotype quantification directly
supports downstream clinical and research decision-making. Because HLA regions are highly
polymorphic and sequencing coverage is often uneven, uncertainty-aware quantification is
critical: overconfident point estimates can silently propagate errors into downstream
analyses, while Orthanq's probabilistic output makes the confidence level explicit.
